Informed Consent for Treatment

The details of the procedure(s) consented to above have been explained to me in terms I understand. Alternative methods and their benefits and disadvantages have been explained to me.

Risks & Complications

I understand and accept the most likely risks and complications of the injections consented, which include but are not limited to:

  • Bleeding
  • Infection or allergic reaction
  • Trauma to nerves or dura
  • Vasovagal reactions (dizziness/fainting, sweating, nausea, vomiting)
  • Spinal headache
  • Soft tissue swelling, bruising, or hematoma formation

I understand and accept that there are complications, including the remote risk of death or serious disability, that exist with any surgical procedure.

Anticipated Outcomes

I understand and accept the anticipated outcomes of the injections I have consented to, including:

  • Pain reduction
  • Temporary increased muscle spasm
  • Temporary post-injection site pain/soreness
  • Temporary elevation of blood sugar (only if injected with steroid medication)

Patient Acknowledgements

I have informed the physician of all my known allergies. I have informed the physician of all medications I am currently taking, including prescription drugs, over-the-counter remedies, herbal therapies and supplements, aspirin, and any other recreational drug or alcohol use. I have been advised whether I should avoid taking any or all of these medications on the days surrounding the procedure.

I have been informed of what to expect in the post-injection period, including but not limited to: estimated recovery time, anticipated activity level, and the possibility of additional procedures. The physician has answered all of my questions regarding this procedure.

Authorization

I authorize my physician, with associates or assistants of his/her choice, to perform the procedure. I further authorize the physician(s) and assistant(s) to perform any other procedure that in their judgment may be necessary or advisable should unforeseen circumstances arise during the procedure.
